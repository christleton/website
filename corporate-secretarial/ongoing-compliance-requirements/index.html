Ongoing Compliance Requirements

There are certain ongoing compliance requirements under the Companies Act for companies incorporated in Singapore.

Companies incorporated in Singapore are required to comply with certain ongoing compliance requirements under the Companies Act (“CA”), such as:

  1. First Meeting of the Board of Directors;
  2. Company Common Seal;
  3. Memorandum of Association;
  4. Return of Allotments;
  5. Notice of Alteration in Share Capital;
  6. Notice of Transfer of Shares;
  7. Issue of Share Certificates;
  8. Register of Charges;
  9. Registered Office of the Company;
  10. Publication of Company Name and Registration Number;
  11. Register of Directors’ Shareholdings;
  12. Appointment of Company Secretary;
  13. Register of Directors, Managers, Secretaries and Auditors;
  14. Annual General Meetings;
  15. Minute Books;
  16. Annual Return;
  17. Accounting Records; and
  18. Appointment of Auditor.

This list of compliance requirements under the CA is meant as an introduction and not (nor intended to be) an exhaustive list of all of the requirements under the CA. The information contained herein has been vastly simplified and there are numerous other provisions in the CA (and other relevant legislations) not covered herein.

To ensure compliance with applicable laws, you should seek professional legal advice.

(1) First Meeting of the Board of Directors

As soon as reasonably practicable following incorporation and preferably within 1 month of incorporation, the company directors should convene a first meeting of the board, noting and resolving certain matters, such as:

  • the incorporation of the company;
  • the first directors;
  • the company secretary;
  • the registered office;
  • the common seal;
  • the issue of share certificates to the initial shareholders; and
  • setting the first financial period and the financial year end.

(2) Company Common Seal

Every Singapore company should have a common seal, which should be adopted at the company’s first board meeting.

Under section 123 of the CA, the common seal is required to seal the share certificates, which must be issued (according to section 130(1) of the CA):

  • if it is an allotment, within 2 months of such allotment; or
  • if it is a transfer, within 1 month of such transfer being lodged with the company.

Duplicate Common Seal

Under section 124 of the CA, a company may (if so authorised by its constitution) have a duplicate common seal which shall be a facsimile of the common seal of the company with the addition on its face of the words “Share Seal”. A certificate under such duplicate seal is deemed to be sealed with the common seal of the company.

(3) Memorandum of Association

Under section 22(4) of the CA, a copy of the company’s memorandum of association must be kept at the registered office of the company.

Such memorandum of association must be duly signed by the subscribers and state (if the company is to have a share capital) the number of shares that each subscriber has agreed to take.

(4) Return of Allotments

Under section 63 of the CA, whenever a company makes any allotment of shares (other than a deemed allotment), the company must lodge within 14 days lodge with the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority (“ACRA”) a return of the allotments stating:

  • the number of shares comprised in the allotment;
  • the amount (if any) paid or deemed to be paid on the allotment of each share;
  • the amount (if any) unpaid on the allotment of each share;
  • if the company has different share classes, the class of shares to which each share comprised in the allotment belongs; and
  • for each of the shareholders (or if the company will have more than 50 shareholders after such allotment, for each of the 50 shareholders who following the allotment will hold the most number of shares in the company (excluding treasury shares)):
    • the full name;
    • identification;
    • nationality;
    • address; and
    • the number and class of shares held.

Definition of “identification”

Under section 63(4) of the CA, “identification” means, in the case of a person issued with an identity card, the number of his identity card and, in the case of a person not issued with an identity card, particulars of his passport or such other similar evidence of identification as is available.

(5) Notice of Alteration in Share Capital

Under section 71 of the CA, where any of the following alteration of share capital has taken place, a company may lodge with ACRA a notice of the same in the prescribed form:

  • consolidate and divide all or any of its share capital;
  • convert all or any of its paid-up shares into stock and reconvert that stock into paid-up shares;
  • subdivide its shares or any of them, so however that in the subdivision the proportion between the amount paid and the amount (if any) unpaid on each reduced share must be the same as it was in the case of the share from which the reduced share is derived;
  • cancel the number of shares which at the date of the passing of the resolution in that behalf have not been taken or agreed to be taken by any person or which have been forfeited and diminish the amount of its share capital by the number of the shares so cancelled.

Timeframe for Filing

Although no time frame is set for the filing of the notice of alteration in share capital, it must be lodged before the annual return is filed.

(6) Notice of Transfer of Shares

Under section 128A of the CA, where there has been a transfer of shares, a company may lodge with ACRA a notice of such transfer in the prescribed form.

The notice must state:

  • every other transfer of shares effected prior to the date of the notice (other than a transfer that has been previously notified to ACRA); or
  • if the company has more than 50 members after the transfer, the prescribed information in relation to the shares held by each of the 50 members who hold the most number of shares in the company after the transfer.

(7) Issue of Share Certificates

Under section 130(1) of the CA, every Singapore company must issue share certificates to its shareholders:

  • if it is an allotment, within 2 months of such allotment; or
  • if it is a transfer, within 1 month of such transfer being lodged with the company.

Form of Share Certificate

Under section 123(2) of the CA, every share certificate must:

  • be sealed with the common seal of the company;
  • state the date of issue of the certificate;
  • state the name of the company and the authority under which the company is constituted;
  • state the address of the registered office of the company in Singapore;
  • state the class of shares;
  • state whether the shares are fully or partly paid-up; and
  • state the amount (if any) unpaid on the shares.

Failure to Comply

Although share certificates are prima facie evidence of title to the shares, failure to comply with section 123 of the CA will not affect the rights of any holder of the shares.

However, the company and every officer of such company who is in default will be guilty of an offence.

(8) Register of Charges

Under section 138 of the CA, every company must keep at its registered office:

  • a register of charges; and
  • the instruments (or copies thereof) creating any charge requiring registration under Division 8 of the CA.

Failure to Comply

If there is default in complying with section 138 of the CA, the company and every officer of such company who is in default will be guilty of an offence and, upon conviction, a fine of up to SGD2,000 and a default penalty.

(9) Registered Office of the Company

Under section 142 of the CA, a company must from its incorporation have a registered office, which must be:

  • within Singapore;
  • where all communications and notices may be addressed; and
  • open and accessible to the public for not less than 3 hours during ordinary business hours on each business day.

Office Hours

Under section 143 of the CA, particulars of the registered office must be lodged with ACRA at the time of incorporation (together with the company's constitution), stating:

  • the address and designation of the situation or address of the registered office; and
  • the days and hours during which the registered office is open and accessible to the public.

Change in registered office

If there are any changes to the particulars of the registered office, it must lodged with ACRA within 14 days of any such change. However, no notice of the days and hours during which the office is open and accessible to the public is required if the office is open for at least 5 hours during ordinary business hours on each business day.

(10) Publication of Company Name and Registration Number

Under section 144(1) of the CA, the name of a company must appear in legible romanised letters on:

  • its common seal; and
  • all business letters, statements of account, invoices, official notices, publications, bills of exchange, promissory notes, indorsements, cheques, orders, receipts and letters of credit of or purporting to be issued or signed by or on behalf of the company.

Under section 144(2) of the CA, the registration number of a company must appear in a legible form on all business letters, statements of account, invoices, official notices and publications of or purporting to be issued or signed by or on behalf of the company.

Failure to Comply

If the requirements for the display of the company name or company registration number is not met, the company will be guilty of an offence.

If an officer of a company or any person on its behalf:

  • uses or authorises the use of any seal purporting to be a seal of the company whereon its name does not so appear;
  • issues or authorises the issue of any business letter, statement of account, invoice or official notice or publication of the company wherein its name is not so mentioned; or
  • signs, issues or authorises to be signed or issued on behalf of the company any bill of exchange, promissory note, cheque or other negotiable instrument or any indorsement, order, receipt or letter of credit wherein its name is not so mentioned,

he/she shall be guilty of an offence, and where he/she has signed, issued or authorised to be signed or issued on behalf of the company any bill of exchange, promissory note or other negotiable instrument or any indorsement thereon or order wherein that name is not so mentioned, he/she shall in addition be liable to the holder of the instrument or order for the amount due thereon unless it is paid by the company.

(11) Register of Directors’ Shareholdings

Under section 164 of the CA, a company must keep a register showing each director's particulars of:

  • shares in that company or in a related corporation, being shares of which the director is a registered holder or in which he has an interest and the nature and extent of that interest;
  • debentures of or participatory interests made available by the company or a related corporation which are held by the director or in which he has an interest and the nature and extent of that interest;
  • rights or options of the director or of the director and another person or other persons in respect of the acquisition or disposal of shares in the company or a related corporation; and
  • contracts to which the director is a party or under which he is entitled to a benefit, being contracts under which a person has a right to call for or to make delivery of shares in the company or in a related corporation.

Director's Notice

Under section 165 of the CA, a director must give notice to his/her company of the particulars relating to requirements of section 164 of the CA, and of any changes therein.

Such notice must be given within 2 business days:

  • of the date the director became a director of the company;
  • of the date the director acquired the interest required to be disclosed under section 164; or
  • after the occurrence of the event giving rise to the change.

A company must within 7 days after it receives a notice given by such director, send a copy of the notice to each of the other directors of the company.

Failure to Comply

If a director fails to give the required notice under section 165 of the CA, he/she is guilty of an offence and will on conviction be liable to a fine not exceeding SGD15,000 or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 3 years and, in the case of a continuing offence, to a further fine of SGD1,000 for every day during which the offence continues after conviction.

(12) Appointment of Company Secretary

Under section 171 of the CA, every company must have at least 1 secretaries. Each of the secretary must:

  • be a natural person (that is, a human being and not a corporation or legal entity);
  • have his/her principal or only place of residence in Singapore;
  • have the requisite knowledge and experience to discharge the functions of secretary of the company; and
  • not also be the director, if the company only has one director.

The office of company secretary cannot be vacant for more than 6 months at any one time.

Qualification of Company Secretary

It is the directors' duty (and not the company secretary's) to ensure that the appointed Company Secretary have the requisite knowledge and experience to act as a company secretary.

If the company is a public company or is required by ACRA to do so, a properly qualified person must be appointed as the company secretary. A qualified person is someone who:

  1. has been a secretary of a company for at least three (3) of the five (5) years immediately before his/her appointment as secretary of the public company;
  2. is a qualified person under the Legal Profession Act of Singapore;
  3. is a public accountant registered under the Accountants Act of Singapore;
  4. is a member of the Institute of Certified Public Accountants of Singapore;
  5. is a member of the Singapore Association of the Institute of Chartered Secretaries and Administrators;
  6. is a member of the Association of International Accountants (Singapore Branch); or
  7. is a member of the Institute of Company Accountants, Singapore.

Company Secretary to be Present at Registered Office

The company secretary must be present at the registered office of the company by himself/herself or his/her agent or clerk on the days and at the hours during which the registered office is to be accessible to the public.

However, if the company is private, the company secretary, his/her agent or clerk need not be physically present at the registered office during the times specified so long as the company secretary, his/her agent or clerk is readily contactable by a person at the registered office by telephone or other means of instantaneous communication during those times.

(13) Register of Directors, Managers, Secretaries and Auditors

Under section 173 of the CA, every company must keep a register of directors, managers, secretaries and auditors at its registered office.

The register of directors, managers, secretaries and auditors must contain:

  • for each director:
    • a signed copy of his consent to act as director with a prescribed statement that he/she is not disqualified to act as a director;
    • his/her present full name (and any former name);
    • his/her residential address;
    • his/her nationality and identification (if any); and
    • documentary evidence (if any) of any change in his/her name;
  • for each manager, secretary and auditor:
    • his/her full name;
    • his/her identification; and
    • address;
  • for each secretary, a signed copy of the consent of the secretary to act as the company's secretary.

Change in Particulars

If there are any changes in the particulars of any director, manager or secretary (including any appointments, resignations and disqualifications):

  • the company must be notified;
  • ACRA must be notified in the prescribed form within 1 month of such change; and
  • the register of directors, managers, secretaries and auditors must be updated accordingly.

Deemed Notification for Change of Residential Address

If a change of residential address has been made under section 8 of the National Registration Act, he/she is deemed to have notified ACRA of the change in residential address in accordance with section 173(7)(b) of the CA.

(14) Annual General Meetings

Under section 175 of the CA, every company must hold a shareholders’ meeting called the ‘annual general meeting’ (“AGM”):

  • once in every calendar year;
  • not more than 15 months after the holding of the last preceding AGM; and
  • provided that a company holds its first AGM within 18 months of its incorporation, it need not hold it in the year of its incorporation or in the following year.

Dispensation of AGM

Notwithstanding section 175 of the CA, a private company may dispense with the holding of AGM by passing a resolution with the unanimous approval (100%) of all shareholders.

If such a resolution to dispense with the AGM is duly passed:

  • it will have effect for the year in which it is made and subsequent years;
  • does not affect any liability already incurred by reason of default in holding an AGM; and
  • the doing of anything at an AGM will instead be done by a resolution by written means under section 184A of the CA.

Failure to Comply

If a company fails to hold its AGM in accordance with section 175 of the CA:

  • the company and every officer of the company who is in default will be guilty of an offence and will be liable on conviction to a fine not exceeding $5,000 and also to a default penalty; and
  • the Court may on the application of any member order a general meeting to be called.

(15) Minute Books

Under section 188 of the CA, every company must keep minutes of:

  • all proceedings of general meetings; and
  • all proceedings of directors’ meetings (and of its managers, if any).

The minute books must contain:

  • resolutions passed by written means under section 184A of the CA; and
  • if the company has only 1 director:
    • the passing of resolutions by that director; and
    • the making of declarations by that director.

These minutes must:

  • be entered into the minute books within 1 month from the date of the meeting or the passing of the resolution;
  • for a section 184A resolution, be signed by a director within a reasonable time after the resolution is passed; and
  • where the company has only one director, that director must sign the minutes within a reasonable time after the resolution is passed or the declaration is made.

Location and Inspection of Minute Books

Under section 189 of the CA, the minutes books must be kept by the company at the company's registered office or the principal place of business in Singapore.

Failure to Comply

If default is made in complying with section 188 of the CA, the company and every officer of the company who is in default will be guilty of an offence and will be liable on conviction to a fine not exceeding SGD2,000 and also to a default penalty.

(16) Annual Return

Under section 197 of the CA, every company having a share capital must lodge with ACRA a return containing:

  • the particulars found in the Eighth Schedule of the CA; and
  • accompanied by the documents set out in regulation 38 of the Companies (Filing of Documents) Regulations.

Confirmations of Particulars in Annual Return

Under regulation 36 of the Companies (Filing of Documents) Regulations, an annual return of a company having a share capital must ensure and confirm:

  • where he/she is an officer of the company, that the particulars of the company referred to in the Eighth Schedule and contained in the return are accurate and up-to-date as at the relevant date; or
  • where he/she is not an officer of the company, that he/she has confirmed with an officer of the company that the particulars of the company referred to in the Eighth Schedule and contained in the return are accurate and up-to-date as at the relevant date.

Under regulation 37 of the Companies (Filing of Documents) Regulations, an annual return of a company having a share capital must contain:

  • a confirmation that an officer of the company has inspected the share register of the company;
  • a confirmation as to whether any transfer of ownership of shares in the company has been registered since the date of the last return of the company or, if none had been lodged previously, the date of incorporation of the company;
  • where the company is a private company (but not an exempt private company), a confirmation that:
    • the company is a private company; and
    • the company does not have more than 50 members.

Timeframe for Filing

Under section 197(4) of the CA, the annual return must be filed within 1 month from the date of a company's Annual General Meeting.

(17) Accounting Records

Under section 199 of the CA, every company must keep such accounting and other records that:

  • sufficiently explains the transactions and financial position of the company;
  • enables true and fair financial statements (and any documents required to be attached thereto) to be prepared from time to time; and
  • are kept in such a manner that can be conveniently and properly audited.

Record Retention Period

Such accounting and other records must be kept for a minimum of 5 years from the end of the financial year in which the transactions or operations to which such records relate are completed.

(18) Appointment of Auditor

Under section 205 of the CA, the directors of a company must appoint an accounting entity (or entities) to be the auditors of the company:

  • within 3 months of incorporation; and
  • at each AGM of the company.

Such appointed auditors will hold office until the conclusion of the company's next AGM.

Timeframe for Filing

Under section 173 of the CA, the company must:

  • lodge with ACRA a return in the prescribed form within 1 month from the date of an auditor's appointment; and
  • update its register of directors, managers, secretaries and auditors.

Exemption from Audit

Although section 205 of the CA mandates that auditors must be appointed for a company, section 205A of the CA exempts certain companies from the obligation to appoint auditors if the company:

  • is a ‘small company’;
  • where the company is a parent company, the parent company is a ‘small company’ and is part of a ‘small group’;
  • where the company is a subsidiary company, the subsidiary company is a ‘small company’ and is part of a ‘small group’; or
  • has been dormant since:
    • the time of its formation; or
    • the end of the previous financial year.

Small Company

Under section 205C and the Thirteenth Schedule of the CA, a company is a ‘small company’ from a financial year if:

  • it is a private company throughout the financial year; and
  • it satisfies any 2 of the following criteria for each of the 2 financial years immediately preceding the financial year:
    • the revenue of the company for each financial year does not exceed SGD10 million;
    • the value of the company's total assets at the end of each financial year does not exceed SGD10 million;
    • it has at the end of each financial year not more than 50 employees.

If a company has not reached its third financial year after incorporation, then the above criteria must be satisfied for each of its immediately preceding financial years.

Small Group

Under section 205C and the Thirteenth Schedule of the CA, a company is a ‘small group’ from a financial year if the group satisfies any 2 of the following criteria for each of the 2 consecutive financial years immediately preceding the financial year:

  • the consolidated revenue of the group for each financial year does not exceed $10 million;
  • the value of the consolidated total assets of the group at the end of each financial year does not exceed $10 million;
  • the group has at the end of each financial year an aggregate number of employees of not more than 50.

Dormant Company

Under section 205B(2) of the CA, a company is dormant during a period in which no accounting transaction occurs. A company ceases to be dormant on the occurrence of such a transaction.
