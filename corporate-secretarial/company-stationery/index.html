Company Stationery

There are certain requirements under Singapore's Companies Act for the display of the company's name and registration number.

The following stationery are usually ordered immediately after incorporation to ensure regulatory compliance and also for business purposes:

  • common seal;
  • rubber stamp; and
  • namecards.

The common seal is required to seal the share certificates and other important documents, such as contracts requiring the common seal.

The rubber stamp, although not a requirement under the Companies Act, can help you comply with section 144 of Singapore's Companies Act (see below).

Requirements for the Company Stationery

Display of Company Name

Under section 144(1) of Singapore's Companies Act, the name of a company must appear in legible romanised letters on:

  • its common seal; and
  • all business letters, statements of account, invoices, official notices, publications, bills of exchange, promissory notes, indorsements, cheques, orders, receipts and letters of credit of or purporting to be issued or signed by or on behalf of the company.

Display of Company Registration Number

Under section 144(1A) of Singapore's Companies Act, the registration number of a company shall appear in a legible form on all business letters, statements of account, invoices, official notices and publications of or purporting to be issued or signed by or on behalf of the company.

If the company stationery lacks the registration number, a rubber stamp of the company with the company registration number is sufficient to meet these requirements.

Offence

The company will be guilty of an offence if the requirements for the display of the company name or company registration number is not met.

If an officer of a company or any person on its behalf:

  • uses or authorises the use of any seal purporting to be a seal of the company whereon its name does not so appear;
  • issues or authorises the issue of any business letter, statement of account, invoice or official notice or publication of the company wherein its name is not so mentioned; or
  • signs, issues or authorises to be signed or issued on behalf of the company any bill of exchange, promissory note, cheque or other negotiable instrument or any indorsement, order, receipt or letter of credit wherein its name is not so mentioned,

he/she shall be guilty of an offence, and where he/she has signed, issued or authorised to be signed or issued on behalf of the company any bill of exchange, promissory note or other negotiable instrument or any indorsement thereon or order wherein that name is not so mentioned, he/she shall in addition be liable to the holder of the instrument or order for the amount due thereon unless it is paid by the company.
