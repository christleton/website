Employment Law in Singapore

There are several legislation governing employment law in Singapore, the main legislation being the Employment Act.

The employer-employee relationship in Singapore is largely governed by the employment agreement entered into between them. Employers and employees are generally free to contract as they wish, although there may be certain limits to this freedom under common law and statutes.

Employment Act

The Employment Act (“EA”) is the main legislation on employment law in Singapore, setting certain minimum standards for employment agreements.

Under the EA, every employment agreement must be at least as favourable to the employee as the conditions in the EA, failing which every such term which is less favourable to an employee is illegal, null and void to the extent that it is so less favourable.

The EA does not cover people who are employed as:

  • managers or executives with monthly basic salary of more than SGD4,500;
  • seafarers;
  • domestic workers; and
  • statutory board employees or civil servants.

Part IV of the EA (which covers rest days, hours of work, annual leave and other conditions of service) only applies to:

  • workmen (whose work involves mainly manual labour) earning a basic monthly salary of not more than SGD4,500; and
  • non-workmen covered by the EA and earning a basic monthly basic salary of not more than SGD2,500.

If you are a manager or executive drawing a salary of SGD4,500 or less (excluding overtime payments, bonus payments, annual wage supplements, productivity incentive payments and any allowance), then you are covered under all parts of the EA except for Part IV.

Managers and executives are employees with executive/supervisory functions, and also includes professionals such as lawyers, doctors and accountants.

Other Legislation

Other legislation relating to employment law in Singapore includes:

  • Central Provident Fund Act
  • Child Development Co-Savings Act
  • Criminal Law (Temporary Provisions) Act
  • Employment of Foreign Manpower Act
  • Employment, Parental Leave and Other Measures Act
  • Enlistment Act
  • Immigration Act
  • Income Tax Act
  • Industrial Relations Act
  • Retirement and Re-employment Act
  • Skills Development Levy Act
  • Trade Disputes Act
  • Trade Unions Act
  • Work Injury Compensation Act
  • Workplace Safety and Health Act

To ensure compliance with the relevant legislation in Singapore, you should seek professional legal advice.
