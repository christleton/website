Record Keeping in Singapore

Records in Singapore are generally required to be retained for at least 5 years, subject to any specific requirements for a longer retention period. More problematic is determining the start of the retention period.

The period for which records and documents must be kept depends on the applicable law such records and documents fall under.

In general, records and documents must be kept for a minimum of 5 years. However, the minimum retention period of 5 years does not necessarily start counting from the records’ date of creation, but from another date.

As such, to ascertain the earliest date records can be destroyed, three (3) key information are required:

  • the applicable law the records fall under;
  • the retention period; and
  • the date the retention period starts counting.

Record Keeping Requirements under Various Governing Law

For ease of reference, the following table is compiled showing some of the record keeping requirements under various governing law:

Applicable Law Record Keeping Requirements
Building Maintenance and Strata Management Act, section 48(2) 5 years from the end of the financial year in which the transactions or operations to which those documents relate are completed.
Business Trusts Act, section 75(2) 5 years from the end of the financial year in which the transactions or operations to which those records relate are completed.
Charities Act, section 12(3)-(4) 5 years from the end of the financial year of the charity in which such records are made.
Companies Act, section 199(2) 5 years from the end of the financial year in which the transactions or operations to which those records relate to are completed.
Corruption, Drug Trafficking and other Serious Crimes (Confiscation of Benefits) Act, section 36(1) (a) if the document relates to the opening of an account with the institution, the period of 5 years after the day on which the account is closed; (b) if the document relates to the opening by a person of a deposit box held by the institution, the period of 5 years after the day on which the deposit box ceases to be used by the person; or (c) in any other case, the period of 5 years after the day on which the transaction takes place.
Customs Act, section 90(2) (a) in relation to goods in a Government warehouse or licensed warehouse, for a period of not less than 5 years from the date those goods are removed from the Government warehouse or licensed warehouse, as the case may be; or (b) in any other case, for a period of not less than 5 years from the date on which the goods are released by the proper officer of customs.
Financial Advisers Act, section 36(5) 5 years from the sending of a circular or other communication.
Goods and Services Tax Act, section 46(2) 5 years from the end of the prescribed accounting period.
Income Tax Act, section 67 5 years from the year of assessment to which any income relates.
Limited Liability Partnerships Act 2005, section 25(2) 5 years from the end of the financial year in which the transactions or operations to which those records relate are completed.
Money-changing and Remittance Businesses Act, section 16(2) 5 years after the day on which the transaction takes place.
Moneylenders Act, section 24(1); Moneylenders Rules 2009, rule 14 5 years from (a) the date the loan is fully repaid; or (b) the date the contract for the loan is otherwise terminated, whether such loan is made or fully repaid, or such contract is terminated, before, on or after 1st March 2009, as the case may be.
Pawnbrokers Act 2015, section 23(2); Pawnbrokers Rules 2015, rule 8 From the time the pawn ticket is issued until the end of 5 years after the pledge is redeemed or forfeited, as the case may be.
Securities and Futures Act, section 102(3) 5 years.
Trust Companies Act, section 28(4) 5 years.
Last updated on 24 October 2015.
