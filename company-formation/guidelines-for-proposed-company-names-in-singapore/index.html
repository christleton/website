Guidelines for Proposed Company Names in Singapore

Before a Singapore company may be incorporated, its proposed company name are subject to certain restrictions and must be approved by ACRA.

In choosing a company name, it is important to note that the selection of company names is also subject to certain restrictions and approvals.

Before incorporating a Singapore company, the proposed company name must be approved by and reserved with the Accounting and Corporate Regulatory Authority of Singapore (“ACRA”).

Reserving a Company Name

A name reservation with ACRA can be filed online and will generally be approved within two (2) days, but may take up to two (2) months if further approvals are required by ACRA or other Governmental authorities.

Once the proposed company name is approved, it will be reserved for an initial period of two (2) months. This is extendable for one (1) more month. During this period whereby the proposed company name is reserved, the company may be incorporated.

Choosing a Company Name

ACRA will reject a proposed company name that is:

  1. identical to that of any other company, limited liability partnership or corporation, or to a business name;
  2. undesirable; or
  3. of the kind the Minister has directed ACRA not to accept.

Identical Company Name

In determining whether one name is identical to another, the following is disregarded:

  1. ‘The’, where it is the first word of the name;
  2. ‘Private’, ‘Pte’, ‘Sendirian’, ‘Sdn’, ‘Limited’, ‘Ltd’, ‘Berhad’ and ‘Bhd’;
  3. the following words and expressions where they appear at the end of the name:
    ‘company’, ‘and company’, ‘corporation’, ‘Incorporated’, ‘Asia’,‘Asia Pacific’, ‘International’, ‘Singapore’, ‘South Asia’, ‘South East Asia’ and ‘Worldwide’;
  4. any word or expression which, in the opinion of ACRA, is intended to represent any word or expression above;
  5. the plural version of the name; and
  6. the type and case of letters, spacing between letters and punctuation marks.

The ampersand symbol ‘&’ is deemed to have the same meaning as the word ‘and’.

As the definition of ‘corporation’ in Singapore's Companies Act includes bodies incorporated outside of Singapore, ACRA may reject a proposed company name if it is confusingly similar or identical to the name of any corporation anywhere in the world.

Undesirable Company Name

A proposed company name is undesirable if it is deemed to:

  1. be of an obscene, vulgar or offensive nature;
  2. possibly offend a friendly state;
  3. offend public policy; and
  4. offend a section of the public or members of a religious group.

Proposed company names deemed undesirable will be rejected by ACRA.

Company Name of the Kind ACRA is Directed not to Accept

Ministerial directions on the kind of company names ACRA should not accept are published in the Government Gazette.

Typically, proposed company names that imply official connections or patronage (such as ‘Ministry’ or ‘Temasek’) are rejected.

What to do if my Proposed Company Name is Rejected?

If a proposed company name is rejected, you can either:

  1. file an appeal with ACRA; or
  2. get the entity whose name ACRA deems too similar to the proposed company name to write a consent letter approving the use of the proposed company name.
